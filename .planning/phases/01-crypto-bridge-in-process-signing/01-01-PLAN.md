<objective>
Establish the greenfield `tsig` crate — pinned/reproducible Cargo scaffold, module seams, and
the clap three-persona CLI skeleton — and land the highest-leverage structural control first:
the ONE canonical frost→rust-bitcoin key bridge, pinned by an externally-auditable BIP341/BIP86
known-answer vector covering both an even-Y and an odd-Y-origin key (KEY-03), plus the
`tsig address --pubkey <file>` command that reads a public-artifact file and prints the P2TR
address (KEY-04).

Purpose: the bridge is where three convention systems collide (frost serialization, BIP340
x-only/parity, BIP341 tweak); a silent parity/tweak error here yields an unspendable or
wrong-key signature. Pinning it to the BIPs' own vectors on day one is the cheapest, highest-trust
control ("100 people must verify what they run").
Output: a compiling crate skeleton, the canonical bridge functions, and a committed KAT.
</objective>
